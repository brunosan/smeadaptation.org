Directrices

Vamos a examinar las vulnerabilidades en 5 sectores fundamentales para el bienestar humano ante el cambio climático y otras fuerzas globales. Una vez que estas vulnerabilidades se evalúan, vamos a ver lo que la "preparación" para la inversión en los sectores prioritarios son. Algunas de las células se rellena previamente con base en su estado, otros que tendrán que investigar y determinar usted mismo.

1 Dependencia

¿En qué sector es su empresa particularmente dependientes? Más allá de las operaciones normales, como la iluminación de su oficina o lavar los platos, pero que son esenciales para la producción de sus principales productos y la realización de servicios a sus clientes? Por ejemplo, un fabricante de cerveza sería altamente dependiente del agua como una entrada y una empresa de logística sería altamente dependientes de la infraestructura.

5 Strategy

Now, look back to which sectors have the highest vulnerabilities. T hese are your priority sectors, the sectors in which you should begin building resilience first. How you achieve this resilience will depend on your specific circumstances, but common strategies include investing in more efficient production processes, building a more diverse supply chain and utilizing new technology and information sources to predict potential disruptions.

Second, think about your community and back at Step 2. Unselect the company dependency and only look at the scores for your region. Are they similar or different than your company vulnerabilities? Can you provide products or services that address these vulnerabilities? If your state has high vulnerability to food, your SME might develop more reliable and efficient food delivery or technology distribution systems. High vulnerability in health might indicate an unmet demand for sanitary products.

What is the "readiness" score? Can you make investments easily to build resilience? If so, an aggressive investment strategy in these sectors may be the path forward. If readiness is low, other strategies, such as reaching out to civil society, universities and other businesses to develop cost-effective alliances and solutions to adaptation challenges may be best.

Model

  • A Vulnerability score < 0 is Low, otherwise High
  • A sector's Vulnerability score is defined as Dependency * (Condition-average of Drivers)
  • Combined Vulnerability is the average of all sector risks

  • Dependency scores Neutral_Low_Medium_High correspond respectively to [0,1,2,3]
  • Condition scores Good_Moderate_Bad correspond respectively to [-3,0,3]
  • Drivers scores Low_Moderate_Strong correspond respectively to [1,2,3]

  • The Overall Readiness score is the average of all readiness indicators
