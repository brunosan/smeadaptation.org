SMEs and Adaptation


Adaptation is an imperative. The world is ever changing. People, enterprises and governments are being challenged to prepare for and minimize the effects of growing changes in climate, population, urbanization, resource availability and other “global forces” that challenge economic development locally and globally. Decreasing water supplies, shifting agricultural patterns, larger coastal populations at risk from flooding and disrupted energy and transport systems are but a few of these challenges.

In Mexico, small-and-medium-size enterprises will be key in ensuring that human and economic development continue in the face of increasing natural disasters, growing coastal populations and resource scarcity. As SMEs provide at least 20% of Mexico's GDP and employ approximately one-quarter of all laborers, building resilience within the SME supply chain and encouraging SMEs to invest in sectors crucial to helping communities adapt is essential.

The SME Adaptation project will help SMEs in Mexico build this resilience by providing resources, a platform for information sharing amongst entrepreneurs, data on vulnerability and investment opportunities and a set of adaptation guidelines. This project will also bring awareness to the government officials, corporate leaders and NGOs on the need to engage with SMEs and facilitate this much-needed investment.

Partners

We are a higher education institution that educates students to become responsible citizens who trigger the development of their communities. We promote in our students humanistic values, an international perspective, and an entrepreneurial culture. We are present throughout México with our 31 campuses and in several other countries through our sites and liaison offices.

ND-GAIN

ND-GAIN's mission is to enhance the world's understanding of the urgency for adaptation to climate change and other global forces and for the support needed through private and public investments for developing countries. Through the open-source GAIN Index, demonstration projects and outreach with businesses, government leaders and other NGOs, GAIN is showing how the private sector can bring about change to help those most vulnerable adapt.

This project is possible due to the generous support of the The John Templeton Foundation.
