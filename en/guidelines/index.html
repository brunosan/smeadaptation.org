Determine Company Exposure

Determine in what sectors your business is at risk.

Disruption in any of these sectors can pose a risk to your business, even if your enterprise only creates products and services in one. Further, changes in one sector can affect the quantity or quality of another (e.g. a lack of water can negatively impact power generation). Thus, it is important for your business to take a holistic look at each sector individually and in connection with each other. The sectors covered in these guidelines include:

  • Energy
  • Water
  • Food
  • Infrastructure
  • Health
First, look for data that reflect changes or directly report on conditions for each sector. This data will not be available for your business or likely your town or immediate community. However, there is data available for many of these sectors at the state and national level:

1.a Direct Data

Find national sector data here: www.smeadaptation.org/national/sector
Find state sector data here: www.smeadaptation.org/state/sector

Find the data you need? How is Mexico performing, how is your state performing on each sector? Depending on the extent of your supply chain, you will need to examine conditions at several geographic scales.

1.b Indirect Data

When there is no direct data available for a sector, you can look for other factors that can affect these sectors. For instance, precipitation patterns will impact agriculture and water levels. An increase in natural disasters will damage infrastructure and energy production. Use this table to link these data to the key sectors:

Research into these factors can help inform your judgment on sectors. To find this data, visit these links:

Find national sector data here: www.smeadaptation.org/national/data
Find state sector data here: www.smeadaptation.org/state/data

1.c Independent research

When data has not been collected or is incomplete, contacting government agencies, NGOs, academic institutions or other businesses/consortiums can help complete the picture.

For ongoing recommendations, stories and resources, visit the Adaptation Forum at www.smeadaptation.org

1.d Compare with other SMEs

What do other SMEs consider risks in your state and country? Visit www.smeadaptation.org/survey to determine what other SMEs believe to be the most significant risks.

After going through these four steps, determine which sectors are risks to YOUR business.

2. Prioritize

Which sectors did you mark as high or medium risk?

2.a Immediacy

Can you determine the immediacy of action for each of these? In other words, will it be 10, 20 or 50 years before changes in these sectors will require attention? If not explicitly stated in the data, utilize the resources discussed in Step A.

2.b Importance

Ask questions such as:

• Can disruption in my current reliance on this sector be easily replaced?
• Is my business completely reliant on the steady functioning on this sector?
• Can I easily relocate if my infrastructure is damaged?

2.c Cost

Ask questions such as:

• Will I need to spend money on built infrastructure, energy or technology?
• Can I adjust only through implementing new policies and training? If so, will there be labor costs involved?

* While full strategies won’t be developed until step 3, based on your knowledge of your business, estimate the costs. Once strategies are explored further in Step C, you may want to reprioritize action on these sectors.

Addressing the sector with the most points should be your priority. Now that this is established, it is time to develop a strategy to build resilience to risks in this sector. Risks in other sectors should be addressed soon after or simultaneously as your primary risk.

3. Develop Strategy

3.a Explore Solutions

Visit the Adaptation Forum www.smeadaptation.org for examples of strategies in different sectors. Other websites and resources can provide useful information as well.

3.b Capital Investments or Policy Changes

Will the selected strategy/s require capital investments or less expensive internal communication and planning?

Consider the ability to invest and capital costs in your community and/or state? Will investments give a high return? See how your state compares to others at:

www.smeadaptation.org/state/readiness
www.smeadaptation.org/state/readiness/survey

3.c Determine Scope of Engagement

With whom will you engage? You may only need to engage your employees and supply chain, or you might consider how government/regulators can assist in building resilience.

• Internal business operations
o Address considering new supply chains, technological changes, cost-savings

• Peers and associations
o Address knowledge sharing, collaboration and cost-sharing

• Government and policy makers
o Address tax reform, regulatory burdens, unfair subsidies, governance and corruption
