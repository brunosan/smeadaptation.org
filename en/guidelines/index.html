Guidelines

We will examine vulnerabilities across 5 sectors crucial to human well-being in the face of climate change and other global forces. Once these vulnerabilities are assessed, we will see what the “readiness” for investment in your priority sectors are. Some of the cells will be pre-populated based on your state, others you will need to research and determine yourself.

5 Strategy

Now, look back to which sectors have the highest vulnerabilities. T hese are your priority sectors, the sectors in which you should begin building resilience first. How you achieve this resilience will depend on your specific circumstances, but common strategies include investing in more efficient production processes, building a more diverse supply chain and utilizing new technology and information sources to predict potential disruptions.

Second, think about your community and back at Step 2. Unselect the company dependency and only look at the scores for your region. Are they similar or different than your company vulnerabilities? Can you provide products or services that address these vulnerabilities? If your state has high vulnerability to food, your SME might develop more reliable and efficient food delivery or technology distribution systems. High vulnerability in health might indicate an unmet demand for sanitary products.

What is the "readiness" score? Can you make investments easily to build resilience? If so, an aggressive investment strategy in these sectors may be the path forward. If readiness is low, other strategies, such as reaching out to civil society, universities and other businesses to develop cost-effective alliances and solutions to adaptation challenges may be best.

Several strategies exist according to the vulnerability or opportunity present in each sector. Strategies can include:

  • Investments in services or products - the SME can seek to expand in its current market by identifying specific services and products that can help communities adapt. Much of this will require new financial investments.

  • Change in internal practices - creating more efficient processes within the business that save energy, water and other resources. Some processes may require little capital investment while others may require installation of new machinery and technology.

  • Collaboration - reaching out to NGOs, other businesses and the government to develop solutions.

  • Gather information - there still may be no apparent path forward. New technologies and approaches may be needed. Check back with smeadaptation.org for solutions.

Visit the SME Adaptation Blog for examples of how similar entrepreneurs are addressing risks and finding opportunities in building resilience.
